The questions people ask before installing anything.

## Rules and risk

### Is modding League of Legends allowed?

Riot's Terms of Service prohibit modifying the game client. Cosmetic mods are
widely used and Riot has historically not pursued action over client-side
custom skins, but that is an observation about the past, not a permission and
not a guarantee. There is no official exemption, and Riot can change its
position or its detection at any time.

Read [Riot's Terms of Service](https://www.riotgames.com/en/terms-of-service)
and decide for yourself. If you are not willing to accept some risk to the
account you are playing on, do not mod it.

### Will I get banned?

Nobody outside Riot can answer this honestly, and you should distrust any source
that claims certainty in either direction. What we can tell you is what the
tools actually do:

- Mods documented here replace **assets only** - models, textures, animations,
  sounds, text.
- The [patcher](/start-here/how-modding-works/) redirects the game's file reads.
  It does not alter game logic or change what your client sends to Riot's
  servers.
- Nothing here gives a competitive advantage. There is no vision change, no
  automation, no scripting.

That is a meaningfully different category from cheat software. It is not a
promise about enforcement.

### Do other players see my mods?

No. Everything is local to your machine. A custom skin is rendered only on your
client; every other player sees the default.

### Is it safe to use on my main account?

That is a risk decision only you can make, and it depends on how much the
account matters to you. See the two answers above - we are not going to tell you
it is safe.

## Account and machine safety

### Should a mod ever ask for my Riot login?

**No. Never.** No mod, and no LeagueToolkit tool, needs your Riot account
credentials for anything. Anything asking for them is trying to steal your
account - close it and report the source.

### Are mods themselves dangerous to my computer?

A `.modpkg` or `.fantome` is a data archive, not a program. Installing one does
not execute code from the mod author.

The real risk is being handed something that **isn't** a mod - an `.exe`, an
installer, or a "loader" dressed up as one. See
[Checking a download](/start-here/finding-mods/#checking-a-download).

### Does modding damage my game installation?

No. LTK Manager never modifies your League files. It builds a separate
[overlay](/start-here/how-modding-works/) and the patcher redirects reads to it
at runtime. Stop the patcher and the game reads Riot's original files again -
there is nothing to undo or repair.

If you ever want to be certain, Riot's client has its own repair function, but
in normal use you will not need it.

## Using mods

### Do I need to keep LTK Manager open while playing?

The patcher has to be running for mods to be active. Start it before you launch
the game and leave it running.

### Why aren't my mods showing up in game?

In order of likelihood:

1. The mod isn't enabled.
2. The patcher isn't running.
3. You enabled the mod _after_ starting the patcher.

That last one catches everybody - the overlay is built when you press
**Patch**, so changes afterwards need a stop and re-patch.

Full list in [Troubleshooting](/using-mods/troubleshooting/).

### Can I use several mods at once?

Yes. When two mods change the same file, load order decides which one wins -
mods higher in your library take priority. Two skin mods for the _same_ champion
skin will conflict; mods for different champions coexist fine.

See [Managing Your Library](/using-mods/library/).

### Can I switch between different sets of mods?

Yes - that is what [profiles](/using-mods/profiles/) are for. Each profile has
its own enabled mods and load order.

## Updates

### Do mods survive a League patch?

Your library does; your overlay does not. After a game patch you re-apply, and
some mods will need their author to update them. See
[Updating After a Patch](/using-mods/patch-updates/).

### Do I have to update LTK Manager every patch?

Not usually. The manager only needs updating when League changes something the
patcher depends on. When that happens it is announced in the
[Discord](https://discord.gg/B36wgabjmD).

## Platforms and migration

### Does this work on macOS or Linux?

Windows is the supported platform today. macOS support is targeted for
Q2/Summer 2026. Linux support is planned without a timeline, and it covers
only the Creator Workshop and the features it depends on - the patcher will
never be supported on Linux, so you can make mods there but not play with
them. See [Useful Links](/community/links/) for the current roadmap.

### I already use cslol-manager. Do I have to switch?

cslol-manager is deprecated in favor of LTK Manager, and your existing mods
come across. See [Coming from cslol-manager](/start-here/from-cslol/).

### Where do I get help?

The [LeagueToolkit Discord](https://discord.gg/B36wgabjmD) is the fastest
route. Check [Troubleshooting](/using-mods/troubleshooting/) first, and bring
your log file - the page tells you where to find it.
